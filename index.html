Morné van Wyk, PhD

Biochemistry | Systems Biology | Computational Biology

I completed my PhD in Biochemistry at the University of Stellenbosch, South Africa, in January 2024, focusing on computational and experimental biochemistry. My research specializes in dynamical biological systems, particularly metabolic oscillations in yeast cells and calcium oscillations in pancreatic β-cells.

Combining wet-lab techniques with computational modeling, I develop mathematical models to simulate biological phenomena, validate them with real-world data, and contribute to drug target discovery.

My interests also extend to machine learning applications in computational biology, including the development of predictive models for drug discovery, biomarker identification, and precision medicine.

Projects

  • Pancreatic β-cell Metabolism and Synchronization - Investigating the crucial role of pancreatic β-cell metabolism in linking cellular processes to insulin secretion and understanding its synchronization in maintaining healthy pancreas function.

  • Yeast Glycolytic Oscillations - Exploring how glycolysis exhibits oscillatory behavior, controlled by phosphofructokinase, to gain deeper insights into glucose metabolism.

Research & Publications

  • Kinetic Modelling of Glycolytic Oscillations - Published in Essays in Biochemistry, this study explores the mathematical modeling of metabolic oscillations and their implications in cellular function.

Tools

  • Cell Detection - A fluorescent microscopy image processing tool that segments and extracts intensity data from individual cells for further analysis.

  • Antimicrobial Peptide Finder - A web-based screening tool utilizing machine learning models to predict antimicrobial activity in protein sequences.

  • TinyFit - A lightweight Python package implementing linear regression using gradient descent, featuring feature scaling and multi-variable regression capabilities.
